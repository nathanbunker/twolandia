Safety Instructions

Bunker Family Tours 2019

Exploring the deserts of the southwest can be a very safe activity if some general rules are followed. Here is a quick description of the potential dangers you will face and how to manage them.

Heat and Sun

If you haven’t already noticed, it’s hot here! But what you may not know is that the primary source of the heat is the strong sunlight that shines all day, the air itself is relatively cool. Your first step in keeping cool is to find ways to keep the sun off your skin. It seems counter-intuitive, but you will stay cooler in a long-sleeve cotton shirt than a tank-top. We have seen many tourists miserably burning in the sun because they thought this would keep them cool.

The second need will be to drink a lot of water and to eat food that contains salts. (If you drink too much water but not enough salt you can cause serious medical problems for you!) You should always have at least one liter of water with you and carry additional water in your car. Another good idea is to carry a cheap spray bottle with water to squirt yourself, and to take breaks to get wet in streams or other water.

Rocks and Terrain

Now that you are keeping cool, you need to watch where you are putting your feet! The canyons and mountains in this area are actively eroding away. A large rock may look stable but could shift when you step on it. Cliffs may look safe but may be unstable at the edge. You are responsible for watching where you go and not putting yourself in dangerous places!

We can’t tell you what you may or may not be able to manage, and your physical fitness level is not the same as having skill for these situations. We have seen marathon runners who struggle with balancing on rocks while an average looking 60-year-old walks through the same area without any thought. You will need to be aware and be willing to ask for help if you don’t know how to navigate a particular area.

If you have children, it is your responsibility to know their limitations and capabilities. Small children will need to have their hands held by an adult in certain areas.

Cactus and Plants

You will need to watch where you place your feet, mostly because you need to try and not trample on the natural vegetation. (You need to give plants a chance to grow!) But you could also step into a cactus and get some thorns in you. There is not a lot cactus, but you will need to watch each step you take.

Snakes and Spiders

Snakes come out when the weather gets cooler, especially during the evenings of summer days. The first thing you must understand is that snakes do not want to see you and so will try to hide if they hear you. But you should keep your eye out, especially if walking by yourself or up front. If you see a snake, just give it space and don’t bother it. As for spiders and insects as long as you are not sticking your fingers under rocks or into holes you should not have a problem with them.
